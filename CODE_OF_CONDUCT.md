# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free, respectful, and welcoming experience for everyone — regardless of age, background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This is a free and open-source learning repository. Our goal is to build an inclusive environment where beginners, students, testers, and experienced engineers can all learn, contribute, and grow together.

---

## Our Standards

The following behaviors are expected and encouraged from all participants in this community:

- Using welcoming, inclusive, and respectful language at all times
- Being patient and supportive toward beginners and those learning
- Respecting differing opinions, approaches, and levels of experience
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback with professionalism and kindness
- Acknowledging mistakes openly and learning from them without blame
- Focusing on what is best for the community and for learners using this resource
- Crediting contributors fairly for their ideas and work

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- Use of sexualized language, imagery, or any form of unwanted sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting remarks, personal attacks, or derogatory comments
- Public or private harassment of any community member
- Publishing private information of others without their explicit consent
- Dismissing, belittling, or discouraging contributions from beginners
- Deliberate intimidation or creating an unwelcoming atmosphere
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying acceptable standards of behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any conduct that violates this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, documentation, issues, and any other contributions that do not align with these standards. They may also temporarily or permanently restrict any contributor whose behavior is deemed harmful, threatening, or disruptive to the community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies across all project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repository: issues, pull requests, commit messages, and discussions
- Any communication channel associated with this project
- Public representation of this project by contributors or maintainers

---

## Open Source Commitment

This repository is completely free and open source. It is built for learners, students, testers, and developers at all skill levels. Every person is welcome to:

- Use the code, documentation, and examples freely for personal or professional purposes
- Fork the repository and build upon it without restriction
- Share it with colleagues, students, classrooms, or online communities
- Contribute improvements, bug fixes, new content, or corrections
- Raise issues, ask questions, and suggest enhancements

No permission is needed beyond what is stated in the LICENSE file. There is no fee, no registration, and no restriction on reuse or redistribution.

---

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

- Opening an issue in this repository describing what occurred
- Contacting the maintainer directly through their GitHub profile

All reports will be reviewed with confidentiality, care, and without judgment. The identity of the person reporting will remain private. The maintainer is committed to reviewing all reports promptly and taking appropriate action.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. A private written warning from the maintainer explaining the nature of the violation
2. A temporary restriction from contributing to or interacting with the repository
3. A permanent ban from all participation in the project

All enforcement decisions will be made fairly, consistently, and in proportion to the severity and context of the reported behavior.
